Bellabeat Data Analysis Case Study

Exploring fitness smart device usage patterns to unlock growth opportunities for women's health technology

Bellabeat Study

Project Overview

Bellabeat is a high-tech manufacturer of health-focused smart products designed specifically for women. This analysis explores consumer usage patterns of non-Bellabeat smart devices to uncover insights that could help guide Bellabeat's marketing strategy and product development.

Objectives

  • Analyze smart device usage data to discover trends in how consumers use fitness trackers
  • Identify opportunities for Bellabeat to grow as a company in the women's health tech market
  • Provide high-level recommendations for Bellabeat's marketing strategy based on data insights
  • Examine correlations between activity levels, sleep patterns, and overall wellness

Tools Used

SQL (BigQuery) Python (Pandas) Data Visualization (Matplotlib, Seaborn) Tableau Google Sheets

Data Formatting and Cleaning

Data Cleaning and Validation

-- Add a new column for parsed datetime  
        ALTER TABLE hourlycalories_merged ADD COLUMN  
            ActivityDateTime DATETIME;  
                    
-- Update the new column using STR_TO_DATE  
        UPDATE hourlycalories_merged hm  
            SET ActivityDateTime = STR_TO_DATE(ActivityHour, '%m/%d/%Y %l:%i:%s %p');  
                    
-- Check for NULLS  
        SELECT *  
        FROM hourlycalories_merged hm  
        WHERE Id IS NULL OR ActivityHour IS NULL OR  
            Calories IS NULL;  
                    
-- Count duplicate rows  
        SELECT Id, ActivityHour, COUNT(*)  
        FROM hourlycalories_merged hm  
        GROUP BY Id, ActivityHour  
        HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;

Average Calories Burned by Time of Day

Analyzing how calorie expenditure varies throughout different parts of the day:

-- This query groups average calorie burn by custom parts of the day:
    -- Early Morning (5 AM–11 AM), Afternoon (12 PM–5 PM), Evening/Night (6 PM–12 AM)
    
    SELECT
      CASE
        WHEN HOUR(ActivityDateTime) BETWEEN 5 AND 11 THEN 'Early Morning'       -- 5 AM to 11 AM
        WHEN HOUR(ActivityDateTime) BETWEEN 12 AND 17 THEN 'Afternoon'          -- 12 PM to 5 PM
        WHEN HOUR(ActivityDateTime) BETWEEN 18 AND 23 THEN 'Evening/Night'      -- 6 PM to 12 AM
        ELSE 'Other'                                                            -- 12 AM to 4 AM
      END AS time_of_day,
      ROUND(AVG(Calories), 2) AS avg_calories                                   -- Average calories burned
    FROM
      hourlycalories_merged hm 
    GROUP BY
      time_of_day
    ORDER BY
      FIELD(time_of_day, 'Early Morning', 'Afternoon', 'Evening/Night', 'Other')

Sleep Pattern Analysis

Examining sleep patterns and their relationship to activity levels:

-- Average sleep metrics
SELECT
    AVG(TotalMinutesAsleep) / 60 AS avg_hours_asleep,
    AVG(TotalTimeInBed) / 60 AS avg_hours_in_bed,
    AVG(TotalTimeInBed - TotalMinutesAsleep) AS avg_minutes_awake_in_bed
FROM
    `bellabeat-case-study.fitbit_data.sleep_day`;

-- Correlation between sleep and activity
SELECT
    CORR(a.TotalSteps, s.TotalMinutesAsleep) AS steps_sleep_corr,
    CORR(a.VeryActiveMinutes, s.TotalMinutesAsleep) AS vigorous_sleep_corr,
    CORR(a.SedentaryMinutes, s.TotalMinutesAsleep) AS sedentary_sleep_corr
FROM
    `bellabeat-case-study.fitbit_data.daily_activity` a
JOIN
    `bellabeat-case-study.fitbit_data.sleep_day` s
ON
    a.Id = s.Id AND a.ActivityDate = s.SleepDay;

Analysis revealed a mild positive correlation between activity levels and sleep quality, suggesting that Bellabeat could emphasize this connection in marketing materials.

Key Findings

Activity Patterns

Only 27.8% of recorded days meet the recommended 10,000 step goal. Activity levels are approximately 15% lower on weekends compared to weekdays, with Tuesday and Wednesday showing peak activity.

Most users (72%) fall below physical activity guidelines, presenting an opportunity for targeted motivation features.

Sleep Insights

Users average 6.8 hours of sleep, below the recommended 7-9 hours. More active users tend to have better sleep quality, with a positive correlation between steps and total sleep time.

Users spend an average of 39 minutes in bed before falling asleep, indicating potential for sleep hygiene features.

Usage Patterns

Device usage drops by approximately 40% after the first month, suggesting engagement challenges. User activity varies significantly, with top performers achieving more than twice the average step count.

Sedentary time averages nearly 17 hours daily, highlighting an opportunity for movement reminders.

Data Visualization

The visualization above illustrates the weekly activity pattern, with peak activity occurring mid-week and notable decreases during weekends. This pattern suggests an opportunity for weekend-specific engagement strategies.

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Weekend Engagement Program: Develop targeted weekend challenges and social features to boost activity during low-engagement periods. Consider family-oriented activities that make weekend activity more appealing.
  2. Sleep Optimization Features: Enhance sleep tracking with personalized recommendations based on activity patterns. Highlight the connection between physical activity and improved sleep quality in marketing materials.
  3. Retention-Focused User Experience: Implement progressive goal-setting and achievement recognition to maintain engagement beyond the first month. Create a tiered reward system that evolves as users develop consistent habits.
  4. Sedentary Behavior Interventions: Develop gentle movement reminders and micro-exercise suggestions to break up long periods of inactivity. Position these as wellness features rather than disruptions.

These data-driven recommendations can help Bellabeat develop features that address real user needs and pain points, differentiating their products in the competitive women's health technology market.
