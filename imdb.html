IMDb Movie Dataset Analysis

Project Overview

This project explores the IMDb dataset, focusing on movies released between 1894 and 2025. With a total of 135,554 movies and an average user rating of 6.04, the analysis aims to uncover trends in movie quality, viewer preferences, and popularity.

Objectives

  • Analyze trends in movie ratings across different decades
  • Identify genres with consistently high or low viewer ratings
  • Explore correlations between number of votes and average rating
  • Determine the most popular movies by rating and vote count
  • Segment movies by genres to study viewing preferences

Tools Used

SQL Pandas Matplotlib / Seaborn Jupyter Notebook

IMDb Data Cleaning Process Using Microsoft Excel

Data Acquisition and Initial Filtering

Downloaded raw datasets (name.basics, title.basics, and title.ratings) from IMDb's official datasets available at datasets.imdbws.com each containing 1,048,576 rows of data.

Data Structure

Initially, the dataset (Title_basics_movies) consisted of the following columns:

  • tconst (Title Identifier)
  • titleType (Type of Media)
  • primaryTitle and originalTitle
  • isAdult (Adult Content Indicator)
  • startYear and endYear
  • runtimeMinutes
  • genres

Data Cleaning and Filtering

  • Filtered the extensive title.basics dataset specifically to focus on movie entries, narrowing it down to 229,025 relevant entries.
  • Filtered out entries lacking runtimeMinutes information to maintain consistency and accuracy in duration-based analyses.
  • Cleaned the dataset by removing invalid or missing year (/N) entries from the startYear column, ensuring accurate temporal analyses.
  • Removed the unnecessary endYear column, as movies typically do not extend across multiple years.
  • Excluded movies without genres classifications to facilitate clear and meaningful genre-based insights. This resulted in a concise, analysis-ready dataset comprising 161,213 rated movie entries.

These rigorous steps ensured a clean, structured, and enriched dataset, facilitating comprehensive analysis of cinematic trends and viewer preferences.

Movie Counts by Year Range (1890–Present)

--Movie Counts by Year Range (1890–Present)
SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN startYear BETWEEN 1890 AND 1910 THEN '1890-1910'
    WHEN startYear BETWEEN 1911 AND 1930 THEN '1911-1930'
    WHEN startYear BETWEEN 1931 AND 1950 THEN '1931-1950'
    WHEN startYear BETWEEN 1951 AND 1970 THEN '1951-1970'
    WHEN startYear BETWEEN 1971 AND 1990 THEN '1971-1990'
    WHEN startYear BETWEEN 1991 AND 2010 THEN '1991-2010'
    WHEN startYear >= 2011 THEN '2011+'
    ELSE 'Unknown'
  END AS year_range,
  COUNT(*) AS movie_count
FROM `tough-shard-442823-m5.IMDb_Dataset.movies`
GROUP BY year_range

Additional Data to Expand the Analysis

While the IMDb dataset provides comprehensive information about movies and ratings, incorporating additional data sources would allow for deeper insights into viewer behavior and industry trends. These enhancements could help studios and streaming platforms better understand audience preferences:

  • Box Office Revenue: Financial performance data would help correlate ratings with commercial success.
  • Production Budgets: Understanding the relationship between budget and audience reception.
  • Streaming Platform Data: Viewership numbers from platforms like Netflix or Disney+ could reveal consumption patterns.
  • Demographic Breakdown: Age, gender, and location data of voters could help tailor content to specific audiences.
  • Award Nominations/Wins: Correlation between critical acclaim (Oscars, Golden Globes) and audience ratings.

How Could the Team Apply These Insights?

The insights uncovered from the IMDb analysis can inform strategic decisions across content production, marketing, and platform development:

  • Content Strategy: Focus production resources on genres with consistently high ratings (documentaries, biographies) and high engagement (animation, adventure).
  • Marketing Approach: Highlight documentary-style authenticity for prestige films and leverage animation's mass appeal for broader campaigns.
  • Platform Development: Create specialized recommendation algorithms that consider both rating quality (documentaries) and popularity (animation).
  • Talent Acquisition: Partner with directors and actors associated with highly-rated genres and films.
  • Release Scheduling: Time releases of different genres based on their historical performance patterns.
  • Data Analysts: Use the correlation between votes and ratings to refine viewer segmentation and recommendation engines.
  • Catalog Managers: Remove or archive low-performing titles (low rating and low votes) to optimize user experience.

Strategic Recommendations

  1. Invest in Documentary and Biography Content: With documentaries averaging the highest ratings (7.13) and biographies ranking third (6.72), these genres represent opportunities for prestige content that builds brand credibility and attracts discerning viewers.
  2. Leverage Animation's Mass Appeal: Animation scores highly in both average votes (28,008) and ratings (6.42), making it ideal for broad audience engagement. Develop animated content that can cross over to adult audiences while maintaining family appeal.
  3. Reconsider Horror Production Strategy: Despite being one of the most produced genres, horror averages the lowest ratings (5.13). Focus on elevated horror that combines genre elements with higher production values and storytelling.
  4. Revamp Low-Performing Genre Representation: Genres like Game-Show and Talk-Show consistently receive low ratings and votes. Consider limiting their presence on prominent platforms or repositioning them with niche branding.
  5. Develop Weighted Recommendation Algorithms: Use the weighted rating formula (rating × log10(votes)) to surface quality content that balances critical acclaim with popularity, similar to how "The Shawshank Redemption" achieves both.
  6. Use Year-Based Trends to Highlight Nostalgia: Feature classic high-rated films from 1970-1990 in themed campaigns, tapping into the strong performance of titles from these eras.

These recommendations are based on comprehensive analysis of IMDb's movie dataset and can guide content strategy for studios, streaming platforms, and filmmakers looking to maximize both critical and commercial success.

Final Conclusion:

The IMDb analysis reveals clear patterns in movie quality and audience preferences across genres and eras. Documentaries and biographies consistently achieve the highest average ratings (7.13 and 6.72 respectively), demonstrating audience appreciation for factual and authentic storytelling. Meanwhile, animation commands the highest average number of votes (28,008), indicating exceptional mass appeal despite slightly lower average ratings.

The data shows significant genre disparities, with horror films averaging the lowest ratings (5.13) despite their popularity in production volume. This suggests an opportunity to elevate the genre through higher production values and more sophisticated storytelling. The weighted rating analysis confirms that truly exceptional films like "The Shawshank Redemption" achieve both critical acclaim and massive audience engagement.

Temporal analysis reveals the 1990s-2010s as the peak production period, while quality remains consistent across eras for top-tier films. This demonstrates that while production volume fluctuates, the potential for cinematic excellence exists in every era when the right creative elements align.

These insights provide a data-driven foundation for content strategy, helping filmmakers and platforms balance artistic ambition with commercial considerations in an increasingly competitive entertainment landscape.
