CO₂ Emissions Analysis

Project Overview

This Tableau project was developed during the Google Data Analytics specialization on Coursera. It analyzes CO₂ emissions by global regions using public datasets. The focus is on identifying which regions contribute most to global CO₂ levels, how this changes over time, and where positive trends are emerging.

Data Source

The dataset comes from Coursera's guided project resources. It includes:

  • Yearly CO₂ emissions data
  • Region breakdowns
  • Emissions in metric tons

Data-Driven Recommendations

1. Targeted Policy Interventions

  • For high per-capita emitters (Qatar, USA): Implement carbon taxation tied to GDP with revenue funding green infrastructure
  • For fast-growing regions (Asia): Technology transfer programs for renewable energy adoption

2. Energy Transition Roadmaps

  • Model after Europe's success: Create region-specific phase-out plans for coal power plants
  • Accelerate renewables: Set binding targets of 50% clean energy by 2030 for all OECD nations

3. Enhanced Monitoring

  • Real-time emissions tracking: Satellite-based verification systems for all countries >1% of global emissions
  • Standardized reporting: Unified methodology for per-capita calculations including shipping/fuel bunkering

Implementation Timeline: These recommendations should be prioritized according to regional emission profiles, with OECD nations leading implementation within 2-3 years and developing nations following with 5-7 year adaptation periods supported by climate financing.

Conclusion

Global Emissions Inequality

The data reveals a stark divide: while industrialized nations account for 60% of historical CO₂ emissions, developing regions face climate change impacts disproportionate to their carbon footprint.

Positive Trend: 22 European countries have decoupled GDP growth from emissions since 2000.

Policy Effectiveness

Regions with carbon pricing mechanisms (EU, Canada) show 3-5x faster emission reductions than those without, proving policy intervention works.

Opportunity: Scaling successful policies to high-growth regions could prevent 8GT of emissions by 2040.

The Path Forward

Our analysis suggests three critical action areas:

  1. Equitable Responsibility: Top 10 emitting nations should fund 70% of global adaptation costs
  2. Technology Transfer: Accelerate renewable energy adoption in developing economies
  3. Transparent Monitoring: Implement satellite-based verification for all nations >1% of global emissions

Final Thoughts

While the challenge is immense, the data shows meaningful progress is possible. Europe's 22% reduction since 1990 demonstrates that concerted policy action can reverse emissions trends. The next decade presents a narrow window to scale these successes globally.
